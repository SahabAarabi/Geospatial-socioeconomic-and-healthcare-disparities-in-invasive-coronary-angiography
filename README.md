The current study leverages data from a large national cohort of patients undergoing ICA, and employs a novel methodology to evaluate accessibility to IHD care services while accounting for both availability and reachability. Further, we identify counties located in service deserts, and provide actionable recommendations for interventions aimed at mitigating disparities in accessibility. Finally, we develop a provincial-level index of multiple deprivation – capturing the composite variance of several markers of socioeconomic and healthcare development –and assess its association with service utilization, healthcare costs, and burden of disease, in exploratory analyses.
